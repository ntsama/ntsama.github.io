# MSCA Project  
## Cognitive Trapeze: Modeling Language–Thought Dynamics in AI‑Augmented Learning  

---

# Executive Summary  
**Cognitive Trapeze** investigates how **language and thought co‑develop** in AI‑augmented learning environments.  
The project integrates conceptual analysis, cognitive experimentation, multilingual data, and computational modeling to understand how learners navigate between linguistic structures, cognitive representations, and technological mediation.

**Core pedagogical focus:** The project specifically targets **guided reading (lecture suivie)** in priority education zones. It aims to demonstrate how AI-supported multimodal tools help learners bridge the gap between **linguistic decoding** and **deep semantic comprehension**.

**Already-developed pilot materials:** A **digital lesson plan** and an **AI-generated video** for the tale *“La parole vaut contrat”* (6th grade) have already been produced, following the official 5-step guided reading protocol. These resources will serve as the primary corpus for the empirical pilot phase.

Expected outcomes include:  
- a unified theoretical model (Cognitive Trapeze)  
- empirical insights on multilingual and neurodiverse learners  
- an agent‑based simulation of language–thought dynamics  
- open datasets and educational resources  
- Europe–Africa impact in digital education

# PART B1 — EXCELLENCE  

## 1. Project Title  
Cognitive Trapeze: Modeling Language–Thought Dynamics in AI‑Augmented Learning

## 2. Abstract  
This project explores how **language and thought co‑evolve** within AI‑augmented learning environments.  
Building on the *Pedagogical Trapeze & Swing Model* and the **IA4ZEP** field experience, it proposes a dynamic framework to model how learners move between linguistic structures, cognitive representations, and technological mediation. The specific objective is to improve reading comprehension by integrating AI-generated multimodal content (video, audio, adaptive feedback) into guided reading sessions, thereby reducing cognitive load and enhancing semantic access. The project is grounded in the official Cameroonian 6th‑grade French curriculum and follows its 5‑step guided reading methodology.

## 3. Objectives  

### Scientific Objectives  
- Model the dynamic interaction between **language, thought, and technology**  
- Analyze how AI tools influence conceptual development in reading  
- Investigate cross‑linguistic and neurodiverse learning patterns  

### Methodological Objectives  
- Integrate theoretical, empirical, and computational approaches  
- Develop an agent‑based simulation of the reading comprehension process  
- Produce a multilingual dataset of AI‑mediated reading interactions  
- **Align the AI tool with national curricula** and validate its effectiveness in real classroom settings  

### Career Development Objectives  
- Strengthen expertise in cognitive science and computational modeling  
- Build an international research network (Europe–Africa)  
- Publish high‑impact interdisciplinary work

# PART B3 — IMPLEMENTATION  

## Work Packages  

### WP1 — Theoretical Foundations  
Formalization of the Cognitive Trapeze model, integrating philosophy of language and cognitive science. The model will be operationalized through the three feedback loops (short, medium, long) and the concept of embodied knowledge.

### WP2 — Empirical & Experimental Studies  
- **Corpus selection:** Deployment of the *“La parole vaut contrat”* digital lesson, integrating the AI-generated video and following the official 5‑step guided reading methodology.  
- **Prototype deployment:** Testing the MVP app (ASR + comprehension quiz) in 3 pilot ZEP classes in Cameroon.  
- **Data collection:** Measuring progress in decoding fluency, pronunciation accuracy, and comprehension scores.  
- **Iterative refinement:** Adjusting the 90% mastery threshold and feedback modalities based on teacher feedback and learner performance data.  

### WP3 — Computational Modeling  
Agent‑based simulation of the language–thought dynamics during reading comprehension tasks, informed by the empirical data collected in WP2.

### WP4 — Dissemination & Impact  
Publications, workshops, open datasets, and Africa–Europe educational initiatives. The project will also produce a **teacher training module** on AI‑assisted reading instruction.

# Evolutionary Roadmap: MVP → Immersive ROT

The project is designed with a **two‑phase evolutionary strategy**, ensuring realism in the short term and ambition in the long term.

**Phase 1 – MVP (2026–2027): Guided Reading Assistant**  
- Core features: full text reading, ASR feedback, comprehension quiz, 90% mastery threshold.  
- Deployment on Android tablets and smartphones in pilot ZEP classes.  
- Strict alignment with the official 5‑step guided reading methodology.  

**Phase 2 – Immersive ROT (2027–2029): Reading Oral Training**  
- Gradual progression: word → sentence → full text.  
- Gamification: levels, challenges, progress tracking, badges.  
- Multimodal support: short illustrative videos (≤30 sec), adaptive remediation.  
- Backward‑compatible with the MVP architecture via a dedicated progression API.  

This roadmap will be presented to evaluators as a **realistic yet visionary** development path, demonstrating that the project is both feasible today and scalable for the future.
