# Theory  
## The Cognitive Trapeze & Pedagogical Swing Model  

My theoretical work explores the dynamic relationship between **language, thought, and technological mediation**.  
I integrate philosophy of language, cognitive science, linguistics, AI‑augmented learning, and digital literacies.

The central idea is simple:  
**Cognition is movement — not a static state.**

Learners constantly shift between stability (concepts, schemas, prior knowledge) and exploration (hypotheses, interpretations, problem‑solving).  
The Cognitive Trapeze & Pedagogical Swing Model formalizes this dynamic.

---

## 1. The Cognitive Trapeze  
The **Cognitive Trapeze** describes how learners navigate between:

- linguistic structures  
- cognitive representations  
- conceptual anchors  
- technological tools  

Learning is a **trajectory**, not a linear process.

This model explains how AI tools influence:

- conceptual development  
- reasoning strategies  
- metacognitive awareness  
- multimodal understanding  

### The Three Feedback Loops
The Cognitive Trapeze functions as a cybernetic learning system, structured around three nested feedback loops:

- **Short loop (Learner – AI – Knowledge):** Instant correction during oral reading. The AI captures pronunciation via ASR, compares it to the target text, and delivers immediate multimodal feedback (visual, auditory, haptic).
- **Medium loop (Teacher – AI – Learner):** Aggregated performance data (reading time, recurrent errors) is provided to the teacher, who adjusts parameters (text choice, difficulty thresholds) or intervenes directly.
- **Long loop (Teacher – Knowledge – AI):** The teacher selects and sequences content. The AI, using language models and learner corpora, suggests tailored exercises and reorganizations, enabling massive differentiation.

### Embodied Knowledge
The model integrates the concept of **embodied knowledge** (Varela, Thompson & Rosch, 1991). Learning is not merely abstract; it is multi-sensory. The AI presents knowledge through:

- **Auditory modality:** correct pronunciation model, slowed or contrasted playback of the learner's own voice.
- **Visual modality:** dynamically annotated text (highlighted syllables, intonation graphs).
- **Haptic modality:** brief vibrations to mark syllabic rhythm or omissions.

These feedbacks are not merely corrective; they are contingent and adaptive, ensuring redundancy that favors memorization and automatization.

---

## 2. The Pedagogical Swing  
The **Pedagogical Swing** extends the Cognitive Trapeze to educational contexts.  
It describes how teachers and learners co‑construct meaning through:

- scaffolding  
- multimodal explanations  
- adaptive feedback  
- collaborative reasoning  
- AI‑supported mediation  

The swing represents the **rhythm of learning**:  
alternating between stability (understanding) and exploration (discovery).

---

## 3. Contribution to Cognitive Science  
The model provides:

- a unified framework for language–thought dynamics  
- a tool for analyzing AI‑mediated learning  
- insights into multilingual and neurodiverse cognition  
- a bridge between theoretical and applied research  

It addresses a major gap:  
**the lack of integrated models capturing how language and thought co‑evolve in AI‑mediated environments.**
