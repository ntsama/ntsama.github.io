# Research  
## Program of Research & Scientific Vision  

My research explores how **language and thought co‑evolve** in technologically mediated environments.  
I work at the intersection of philosophy of language, cognitive science, linguistics, AI‑augmented learning, and digital literacies.

My scientific program is structured around three major axes:

---

# 1. Language–Thought Dynamics  
Understanding how linguistic structures shape cognitive processes — and how cognitive representations influence language use.

Key questions include:  
- How do learners move between linguistic forms and conceptual structures?  
- How does AI reshape reasoning, interpretation, and meaning‑making?  
- What cognitive mechanisms support multilingual and neurodiverse learning?  

This axis provides the **theoretical foundation** for my work.

---

# 2. AI‑Augmented Learning  
Studying how artificial intelligence transforms learning trajectories, metacognition, and conceptual development.

I investigate:  
- AI‑mediated feedback  
- multimodal explanations  
- adaptive learning environments  
- cognitive scaffolding  
- digital literacies in multilingual contexts  

This axis connects **cognitive science** with **educational innovation**.

---

# 3. Computational Modeling of Cognition  
Developing computational tools to simulate language–thought interactions.

This includes:  
- agent‑based models  
- conceptual networks  
- cognitive simulations  
- multilingual data analysis  
- modeling neurodiverse learning patterns  

This axis provides the **computational backbone** of my MSCA project.

---

# Signature Contribution: The Cognitive Trapeze  
My theoretical model — the **Cognitive Trapeze & Pedagogical Swing** — describes how learners navigate between stability (concepts, schemas) and exploration (hypotheses, interpretations) in AI‑mediated environments.

It integrates:  
- conceptual analysis  
- cognitive experimentation  
- computational modeling  
- multilingual perspectives  

This model anchors my long‑term research vision.

---

# Research Vision  
My goal is to develop a **unified framework** for understanding human cognition in the age of AI — bridging theory, empirical research, and computational modeling across Europe and Africa.
